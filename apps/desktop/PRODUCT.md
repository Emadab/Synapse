# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Anki power users — students in medicine, language learning, and other exam-heavy fields who already rely on spaced repetition daily. They know the algorithm, the workflow, and Anki's quirks intimately; what they're missing is an interface that respects their time. They study in focused sessions, often for hours, and expect the shell to disappear so the cards can carry the session.

## Product Purpose

Synapse is a spaced-repetition flashcard app fully compatible with Anki's format and scheduling (SM-2/FSRS), rebuilt with a modern Tauri + React shell. It exists because Anki's interface has never matched the sophistication of its algorithm. Success looks like power users switching over without losing any capability, while gaining a study experience — and a stats dashboard — that feels considered rather than bolted on.

## Positioning

Anki-compatible, but modern: the same proven scheduling and format users already trust, wrapped in a fast, native-feeling desktop app that Anki never had.

## Brand Personality

Precise, calm, fast. The interface behaves like a well-tuned instrument — no friction, no clutter, quietly confident. It gets out of the user's way during study and earns trust through consistency rather than flourish.

## Anti-references

Not Anki's own UI: the cluttered, inconsistent, dated look that Synapse exists to replace. Not generic gamified study apps either — no mascots, no cartoonish celebration animations, no Duolingo-style gamification. Synapse's confidence comes from precision and speed, not decoration.

## Design Principles

- Respect the session: chrome recedes during study; the card is the interface.
- Compatibility is table stakes, craft is the differentiator — every screen should feel like it was designed, not ported.
- Data earns its place: stats and charts exist to change what a user does next, not to decorate a dashboard.
- Fast beats fancy: motion and effects (glass surfaces, transitions) are restrained and purposeful, never the reason for a delay.
- Quiet confidence over gamification: no mascots, no forced celebration — trust is built through consistency.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG AA: sufficient contrast in both light and dark themes, full keyboard navigation, and respect for `prefers-reduced-motion` throughout (already a pattern in the existing motion tokens).
